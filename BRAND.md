# OpenADLC Brand and Trademark Use

"OpenADLC" and "OpenADLC Verified" are trademarks of StarFang. The code license
([LICENSE](LICENSE)) and the standard license ([LICENSE-STANDARD](LICENSE-STANDARD))
grant copyright rights only. They grant no trademark rights. This page says how you
may and may not use the names and marks.

## You may (no permission needed)
- Say your work "works with OpenADLC", is "built on the OpenADLC standard", or is
  "compatible with OpenADLC". This is factual, nominative use.
- Refer to OpenADLC by name in articles, talks, docs, and comparisons.
- Link to the project and the openadlc.com site.

## You may not (without written permission)
- Name your product, service, pack, fork, company, domain, or social handle
  "OpenADLC" or anything confusingly similar.
- Use the OpenADLC logo or wordmark as your own.
- Imply endorsement, partnership, official status, or that your build is the
  official OpenADLC, when it is not.
- Claim your pack or implementation is "OpenADLC Verified" unless StarFang has
  verified it under the program terms.
- Register trademarks, domains, or handles that are confusingly similar.

## Competing is fine; impersonating is not
Anyone may build their own implementation of the open ADLC standard and compete.
You just cannot call it "OpenADLC", present it as the official build, or claim it
is "OpenADLC Verified". The standard is open; the name is ours.

## "OpenADLC Verified"
This is a quality and conformance mark StarFang controls. It may be applied only to
packs and implementations StarFang has verified, under the program's published
terms. It is an ordinary trademark, not a third-party certification mark.
