# The OpenADLC manifesto

Agentic coding changed the speed of building. It did not change who is responsible for what gets built. A model can write a thousand lines before you finish your coffee. It can also push them to production before you finish your sentence. Speed without a hand on the wheel is not progress. It is a faster way to ship the wrong thing.

We believe:

**The human stays in the loop.** Not as a bottleneck, as the author of intent and the owner of consequence. The agent proposes. The human decides at the points that matter.

**Keep the machine fed.** An agent that finishes a task and waits wastes the speed. Work is fuel: discovery turns intent into a queue of well-formed, dependency-ordered work, so the agent always has the next thing to build, and the lifecycle's checkpoints keep that flow bounded, never a runaway.

**Discipline is the feature, not the friction.** A plan you approved. A review you saw. A checkpoint that stops and asks before anything leaves your machine. The lifecycle is the product.

**Nothing leaves the machine without a yes.** Outbound is a decision, never a default. The lifecycle stops at each outbound boundary and asks for that yes, and it keeps a record of what you approved.

**Mechanism over prose.** If something must happen every time, wire it to a hook, not a paragraph. Suggestions decay. Mechanisms hold.

**Open where it counts.** An open standard with a source-available reference implementation, built on open standards, so your discipline travels with the agent you already use, not the one we sell. The standard is free for anyone; the source is publicly viewable and free for individuals; organizations need a commercial seat.

**Yours to extend, never yours to drift.** Opinionated about the lifecycle, open about your domain and your taste. Bring your own, and keep a maintained, current core underneath.

**Earn the trust, never claim it.** Method as proof. We do not oversell what the lifecycle catches or how good a model is. Correct, current, audited. The work speaks.

Build fast. Keep the wheel. Keep it moving.
